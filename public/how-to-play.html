How to Play

Word Hunt — complete guide

The Goal

Word Hunt is a two-team word-guessing game. The Dawn Guild and the Dusk Guild compete to be the first team to reveal all of their own tiles on the board. Every game features a 19-tile board arranged in a hexagonal pattern — and not all tiles belong to you.

One team's Pathfinder knows exactly which tiles belong to whom. Their job is to guide their Seekers toward the right tiles using nothing but a single word and a number. The Seekers do the rest.

Roles

Every player picks a guild and a role before the game starts. You can switch at any time from the lobby.

Dawn Guild
Pathfinder
Sees the full treasure map — which tiles belong to Dawn, Dusk, Treasure, Abyss, or neither. Gives clues to guide the Seekers. Cannot touch the board.
Dusk Guild
Pathfinder
Same as Dawn's Pathfinder — sees all tile identities and charts the expedition with one-word clues.
Dawn Guild
Seeker
Hunts for the team's tiles based on the Pathfinder's clue. Taps tiles on the board to reveal them. Can use power-ups (if enabled).
Dusk Guild
Seeker
Same as Dawn's Seeker — interprets clues and taps tiles, trying to find Dusk's tiles without hitting anything dangerous.
The Tiles

The board has 19 tiles. Each belongs to one of five types — only the Pathfinders know which is which before the game begins.

D
Dawn Tiles
Belong to the Dawn Guild. Revealing one scores a point for Dawn. Dawn Seekers want to find all of these.
D
Dusk Tiles
Belong to the Dusk Guild. Revealing one scores a point for Dusk. Dusk Seekers want to find all of these.
—
Neutral Tiles
Belong to neither team. Revealing one simply ends your turn — no points scored, no penalty.
💰
Treasure Tile
There is one Treasure tile on the board. Revealing it awards your team a bonus point and your turn continues — a valuable find that can tip a close game. It also acts as a tiebreaker if scores are level at the end.
🌀
Abyss Tile
There is one Abyss tile. Revealing it ends the game immediately — and the opposing team wins. It is the single most dangerous tile on the board. Avoid it at all costs.
How a Turn Works
1
Pathfinder Charts the Course
The active team's Pathfinder enters exactly one word and a number. The word is the clue — it should link to one or more of their team's tiles on the board. The number tells Seekers how many tiles the clue connects to. A count of 0 means "unlimited guesses, use your judgment."
2
Seekers Hunt
The Seekers discuss and tap tiles on the board. You may guess up to the clue number plus one bonus guess. Each correct guess (your own team's tile) reveals the tile and you can keep going. A wrong guess — neutral, opponent's tile, or worse — ends the guessing phase immediately (unless a Shield is active).
3
Take a Nap (End Turn Voluntarily)
At any point during guessing, the active Seekers can choose to "Take a Nap" and voluntarily pass the turn to the other team — useful when you've found the tiles you wanted or are uncertain about the rest.
4
Turns Alternate
After a turn ends — whether by choice, wrong guess, or the turn limit — the other team's Pathfinder takes over. This continues until one team wins.
Winning the Game

A team wins by revealing all of their assigned tiles first. But there are two shortcuts:

Treasure: Revealing the Treasure tile earns your team a bonus point and your turn keeps going. It can also break a tie — if both teams finish level on points, the team that found the Treasure wins.

Abyss: Revealing the Abyss tile ends the game instantly — the opposing team wins, no matter the score. It is an immediate, total loss for the team that uncovers it.

The header always shows the current score and whose turn it is, so everyone stays in the loop at a glance.

Power-ups (Relics)

When Power-ups are enabled in Game Settings, Seekers have access to two relics per game. Each relic can be used once per match.

👁
Peek
Privately reveals the nature of one tile before you commit to guessing it. Only you can see the result — it disappears after a few seconds. Useful when you're torn between two tiles.
🛡
Shield
Activates a protective layer. Your next wrong guess will not end the turn — the tile is revealed, but guessing continues as normal. Spend it wisely on a risky pick.
Game Settings

The Pathfinder (or any player in the lobby) can adjust settings before the game begins via the ⚙ gear icon.

Rapid Mode
Adds a countdown timer to each guessing phase (15–300 seconds, configurable). When time runs out, the turn ends automatically. Can only be toggled in the lobby.
Word Definitions
Pathfinders can hover over any unrevealed tile to see its dictionary definition. Helpful for thinking through connections without having to recall every meaning from memory. Can only be toggled in the lobby.
Power-ups
Enables or disables the Peek and Shield relics for Seekers. Can be changed at any time.
Color Blind Mode
Replaces Dawn red with orange and Abyss dark purple with indigo for clearer distinction. Can be toggled at any time and only affects your own view.
Tips & Strategy
◆As a Pathfinder, think about which of your tiles share something in common — a category, a shape, a feeling — that doesn't accidentally point to the Abyss or the opponent's tiles.
◆A clue count of 0 is legal — it signals "unlimited guesses" and works best when you have confidence in your Seekers and want to let them catch up on old clues.
◆As a Seeker, always guess your most confident tile first. If it turns out to be wrong, the Shield relic can save the turn. Save the Shield for when the stakes are highest.
◆Use Peek on tiles you strongly suspect are yours but aren't 100% sure about. It confirms or denies before you commit — spending one use of Peek is almost always better than handing a point to the enemy.
◆Don't be afraid to Take a Nap early. Stopping after two correct guesses is often smarter than risking a third uncertain one.
◆The Treasure tile gives a bonus point and keeps your turn going — worth hunting for, especially in a close game. The Abyss tile ends the game immediately in the opponent's favor, so when a clue feels uncertain, think twice before guessing.
