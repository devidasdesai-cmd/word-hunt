How to Play

Word Hunt — complete guide

The Goal

Word Hunt is a two-team word-guessing game. The Dawn Guild and the Dusk Guild compete to be the first team to reveal all of their own tiles on the board. Every game features a 19-tile board arranged in a hexagonal pattern — and not all tiles belong to you.

One team's Pathfinder knows exactly which tiles belong to whom. Their job is to guide their Seekers toward the right tiles using nothing but a single word and a number. The Seekers do the rest.

Roles

Every player picks a guild and a role before the game starts. Your role is locked once chosen and cannot be changed — choose carefully. Each guild can have only one Pathfinder, so if that slot is taken you must join as a Seeker.

Dawn Guild
Pathfinder
Sees the full treasure map — which tiles belong to Dawn, Dusk, Treasure, Abyss, or neither. Gives clues to guide the Seekers. Cannot touch the board.
Dusk Guild
Pathfinder
Same as Dawn's Pathfinder — sees all tile identities and charts the expedition with one-word clues.
Dawn Guild
Seeker
Hunts for the team's tiles based on the Pathfinder's clue. Taps tiles on the board to reveal them. Can use power-ups (if enabled).
Dusk Guild
Seeker
Same as Dawn's Seeker — interprets clues and taps tiles, trying to find Dusk's tiles without hitting anything dangerous.
The Tiles

The board has 19 tiles. Each belongs to one of five types — only the Pathfinders know which is which before the game begins.

Dawn
Dawn Tiles
Belong to the Dawn Guild. Revealing one scores a point for Dawn. Dawn Seekers want to find all of these.
Dusk
Dusk Tiles
Belong to the Dusk Guild. Revealing one scores a point for Dusk. Dusk Seekers want to find all of these.
Neutral
Neutral Tiles
Belong to neither team. Revealing one costs your team 0.5 points and ends your turn immediately. Treat them like landmines — only guess when you're confident.
💰
Treasure Tile
There is one Treasure tile on the board. Revealing it awards your team a bonus point and your turn continues — a valuable find that can tip a close game. It also acts as a tiebreaker if scores are level at the end.
🌀
Abyss Tile
There is one Abyss tile. Revealing it ends the game immediately — and the opposing team wins. It is the single most dangerous tile on the board. Avoid it at all costs.
How a Turn Works
1
Pathfinder Charts the Course
The active team's Pathfinder enters exactly one word and a number. The word is the clue — it should link to one or more of their team's tiles on the board. The number tells Seekers how many tiles the clue connects to. A count of 0 means "unlimited guesses, use your judgment."
2
Seekers Hunt
The Seekers discuss and tap tiles on the board. Each correct guess (your own team's tile) scores a point and lets you keep going. A wrong guess — a Neutral or the opponent's tile — deducts 0.5 pts from your team and ends your turn immediately. The Shield relic fully absorbs one wrong guess: the tile is not revealed, no points are deducted, and guessing continues.
3
Take a Nap (End Turn Voluntarily)
At any point during guessing, the active Seekers can choose to "Take a Nap" and voluntarily pass the turn to the other team — useful when you've found the tiles you wanted or are uncertain about the rest.
4
Turns Alternate
After a turn ends — whether by choice, wrong guess, or the turn limit — the other team's Pathfinder takes over. This continues until one team wins.
Winning the Game

The team with the most points when the game ends wins. The game ends in one of three ways:

All tiles revealed: When a team reveals their last tile, the game does not end immediately. The opposing team gets one final turn to respond — their Pathfinder gives a clue, their Seekers hunt, and the game ends after that turn regardless of the outcome. The header will show "Final Turn" so everyone knows what's at stake.

Treasure: Revealing the Treasure tile earns a bonus point and your turn continues. If scores are level at the end, the team that found the Treasure wins the tiebreaker.

Abyss: Revealing the Abyss tile ends the game instantly — the opposing team wins regardless of score. There is no final turn. Tread carefully.

The header always shows the live score and whose turn it is. After the game, the result screen shows the final scores and the reason the winning team won.

Scoring

Every guess matters. Points accumulate throughout the game — margins can be slim, so precision counts as much as speed.

+1
Reveal one of your own team's tiles
+1
3-in-a-row bonus — reveal 3 of your own tiles in a single turn (stacks with the normal +1 per tile)
+1
Reveal the Treasure tile — your turn continues after claiming it
−½
Reveal a Neutral tile or the opponent's tile — turn ends immediately
—
Reveal the Abyss tile — game ends instantly, the opposing team wins regardless of score

Scores can go negative if a team makes enough wrong guesses. The Shield relic fully blocks a bad guess — no reveal, no deduction, guessing continues.

Power-ups (Relics)

When Power-ups are enabled in Game Settings, Seekers have access to two relics per game. Each relic can be used once per match.

👁
Peek
Privately reveals the nature of one tile before you commit to guessing it. Only you can see the result — it disappears after a few seconds. Useful when you're torn between two tiles.
🛡
Shield
Activates a protective layer. Your next wrong guess will not end the turn — the tile is revealed, but guessing continues as normal. Spend it wisely on a risky pick.
Game Settings

The Pathfinder (or any player in the lobby) can adjust settings before the game begins via the ⚙ gear icon.

Rapid Mode
Adds a countdown timer to each guessing phase (15–300 seconds, configurable). When time runs out, the turn ends automatically. Can only be toggled in the lobby.
Word Definitions
Pathfinders can hover over any unrevealed tile to see its dictionary definition. Helpful for thinking through connections without having to recall every meaning from memory. Can only be toggled in the lobby.
Power-ups
Enables or disables the Peek and Shield relics for Seekers. Can be changed at any time.
Color Blind Mode
Replaces Dawn red with orange and Abyss dark purple with indigo for clearer distinction. Can be toggled at any time and only affects your own view.
Tips & Strategy
◆As a Pathfinder, think about which of your tiles share something in common — a category, a shape, a feeling — that doesn't accidentally point to the Abyss or the opponent's tiles.
◆A clue count of 0 is legal — it signals "unlimited guesses" and works best when you have confidence in your Seekers and want to let them catch up on old clues.
◆Wrong guesses now cost 0.5 pts — a guess that feels only 60% certain can easily hurt more than it helps. "Take a Nap" is always a valid move.
◆Save the Shield for your riskiest guess, not your first one. It fully absorbs a bad guess — no reveal, no −0.5 pts, guessing continues — so timing it well can be the difference between a 1-point swing and a 1.5-point swing.
◆Use Peek on a tile you're 70% sure about, not 95% sure. At 95% confidence just guess it. At 70% the Peek is worth spending to avoid the −0.5 penalty and lost turn.
◆When your team is about to reveal their last tile, think about what clue the opposing team might give during their final turn. If they're likely to score big, consider whether to trigger the final turn now or keep guessing for more points first.
◆The Treasure tile gives +1 and keeps your turn going — it's worth chasing, especially when scores are close. The Abyss ends the game instantly with no final turn, so near the endgame treat any uncertain tile as a potential Abyss.
◆As a Pathfinder, think about which of your tiles share something in common that doesn't accidentally point toward Neutral tiles, the opponent's tiles, or the Abyss. A clue that gets 2 safe tiles is better than one that risks a −0.5 trying for 3.
◆Share the room code or invite link with friends so they can join directly. The more hunters, the more lively the debate.
